## 🐘 Elephant Overview

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, known for their intelligence, social behavior, and distinctive physical features.

## 📊 Key Facts

- **Species**: Three main species - African bush elephant, African forest elephant, and Asian elephant
- **Size**: Can weigh up to 6-7 tons and stand up to 13 feet tall
- **Lifespan**: 60-70 years in the wild
- **Habitat**: Various habitats across Africa and Asia

## 🏗️ Physical Characteristics

- **Trunk**: Used for breathing, smelling, touching, grasping, and sound production
- **Tusks**: Modified incisor teeth, used for digging, fighting, and display
- **Ears**: Large ears help with temperature regulation and communication
- **Skin**: Thick, wrinkled skin that protects against insects and sun

## 🧠 Intelligence and Behavior

- Highly intelligent with complex social structures
- Exhibit empathy, self-awareness, and problem-solving abilities
- Live in matriarchal family groups led by the oldest female
- Communicate through vocalizations, body language, and infrasound

## 🌍 Conservation Status

Many elephant populations are threatened due to:
- Habitat loss
- Poaching for ivory
- Human-elephant conflict

Conservation efforts include anti-poaching measures, habitat protection, and community-based initiatives.

## 🔗 Additional Information

Elephants play crucial roles in their ecosystems as ecosystem engineers, creating water sources and pathways for other species.
